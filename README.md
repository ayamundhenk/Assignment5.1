Analysis of drivers accepting coupons.
For this submission, the focus is on Bar and Coffee House coupons.

<h2>Bar Coupons</h2>
<ul>Drivers who accept a bar coupon who go to bars less than or equal to 3 times per month 37.06 % </ul>
<ul>Drivers who accept a bar coupon who go to bars more than 3 times per month 76.88 %</ul>
<ul>Drivers who go to bars greater than 3 times a month vs. those who go less are  2.07 times more likely to accept the coupon</ul>
<br>
<ul>Drivers who accept a bar coupon who go to bars more than once a month and over age 25:  69.52 %</ul>
<ul>Drivers who accept a bar coupon who go to bars more than once a month and over age 25:  39.45 %</ul>
<ul>Drivers who go to bars more than once a month and over the age of 25 vs. all others  1.76 times more likely to accept the coupon</ul>
<br>
<ul>Go to bars more than once a month, had passengers that were not a kid, and were not widowed → Coupon acceptance rate: 71.32%</ul>
<ul>Go to bars more than once a month and are under the age of 30 → Coupon acceptance rate: 67.05%</ul>
<ul>Go to cheap restaurants more than 4 times a month and income is less than 50K → Coupon acceptance rate: 45.72%</ul>
<br>
<ul>Drivers who go to bars more than once a month who are driving around without a kid in their car and not widowed have the highest coupon acceptance rate of the three scenarios</ul>
<ul>Followed by drivers who go to bars more than once a month and under 30.</ul>
<ul>The under 30 drivers makes sense that they can be more spontaneous to go to a bar without much rearranging of their schedules.</ul>
<ul>The folks with a lower income frequenting cheaper restaurant probably do not want to waste money at a bar.  Makes sense that they have the lowest acceptance rate of the three scenarios</ul>
